Bcakground story

By: Costa Nhamoinesu

Costa Sports Pro is an organisation inspired by both the adversities and resilience of youth in the ghettos of Zimbabwe.

Ghettos in Zimbabwe are the most impoverished neighborhoods with low standards of living. They are characterized by overcrowding, and lack of structured living and business facilities. Some children do struggle to go to school because of limited funds and there is also lack of sporting facilities and structured organisations to develop sports. Striving for survival becomes the main aim for most of the people in the ghetto community including the youths. As a result, the youth don’t really pay attention to the sporting talents they might have. Additionally, there is lack of talent promotion and development in the ghetto, and thus talented youth eventually end up lacking recognition on an international scale.

Despite that many of the youth in the ghettos are very talented, only few of them transition to the highest level of football, that is 1st league European football. This gap is clearly evident as the small (but growing) amount of Zimbabwean 1st league players in Europe originate mainly from the ghetto. Thus, although ghetto youths face more challenges when trying to transition to Europe, if they eventually reach Europe, then they are the ones who are more likely to progress to the top leagues in Europe. This shows that despite their adversities, ghetto youth are indeed very resilient.

My personal example: Before transitioning to Europe, I was playing football at the highest level in Zimbabwe. However, when I transitioned to Europe–because of a lack of proper guidance and knowledge–I started off playing football in one of the lowest leagues on the European level platform (7th Polish league). From there I had to fight my way all the way to the highest level (1st league). Everything that I have learnt throughout my football professional career, the stages I have passed through and those milestones I have reached (e.g., currently the Zimbabwean with the most appearances in UEFA club competitions) provide me with an opportunity to convert all that into a profitable and educative channel for the youths back home in Zimbabwe. The formation of a sports management company gives me the opportunity of sharing my football experiences with other ghetto youths in a more structured professional way, in contrast to our street corner conversations. The idea is to convert negative aspects of a professional football career into positive motivations, and transform the downfalls plus the successes into learning tools for the upcoming ghetto youths. The formation of this organisation, “Costa Sports Pro (CSpro)”, also serves as a way of giving back to the community and an exit plan from my professional football career.

I know first hand that there are opportunities for talented ghetto youths, but they need proper guidance, links and education about professional football culture. Hence, my dedication to the enlightening football in the ghetto.

From a Proud Product of the Ghetto,

"Costa"
